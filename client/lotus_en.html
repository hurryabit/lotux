Lotus

A classic, tactical game

Object of the game

The first to stragically move all their game pieces to the finish is the winner.

Before you begin

On player is given 10 white and the other 10 black pieces. Stack the pieces of the same color in the middle of the game board in the following manner:

  • one stack with 4 pieces
  • one stack with 3 pieces
  • one stack with 2 pieces
  • one stack with 1 piece

Playing the game

Decide who goes first an the take turn moving your pieces (for times when you cannot move, see section No possible moves).

You can only move a piece that is on top of a stack or that is the only piece that is left in this stack. The height of the stack determines how far you can move your piece. That is, you can only move your piece as many squares as there are pieces in the stack. Therefore:

  • a piece in a stack with only one piece can be moved one square
  • a piece in a stack with two pieces can be moved 2 squares
  • a piece in a stack with three pieces can be moved 3 squares
  • etc.

Move your pieces forward along the squares on the board. There are two start squares (marked with a L and a R) but only one exit square (the third end of the map). You cannot move pieces backwards.

On your turn, you can stack any of your colored pieces on top of any other single piece or existing stack. After the game starts there is no limit to the number of pieces in a stack.

You can move any of your pieces forward, even if you still have pieces in the start square.

No possible moves

If you have no piece of your color that is on top of stack or by itself on a square, you cannot move one of your pieces. You must skip your turn and wait until one of your pieces is free to move.

Finish

The finish is after the last square on the board. You do not have to land exactly on it to take you piece out of the game. If you have moved a piece into the finish, take it off the board, out of play.

End of the game

The game is over when a player moves all his or her pieces past the finish.
